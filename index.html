This Privacy Policy explains how the mobile application Eight Gates (“the App”, “we”, “us”) handles your information. Eight Gates is a personal tracking app for diary, mood, nutrition, workouts, hydration, habits, tasks and cycle data. We designed it to be local-first: your entries are stored on your device, not on our servers.

2. Our core principle: your data stays on your device

All content you create in the App — diary entries, moods, voice notes, meals, workouts, water and caffeine logs, habits, tasks, appointments, cycle data, weight and profile — is stored locally on your device in an on-device database. We do not operate a user account system and we do not copy or store this content on our own servers.

Because the data is local, you remain in control: you can export it, import it, or remove it at any time by deleting entries or uninstalling the App.

3. When data leaves your device

Some optional features require sending specific data to third-party services to work. This only happens for the action you trigger, and only with the data needed for that action:

FeatureWhat is sentRecipient / processorPurpose
AI analysis & AI reports The entries of the period you select (e.g. diary, meals, workouts, hydration), or the question you ask Google (Gemini API) To generate the requested analysis or answer
AI food photo recognition The food photo you take Google (Gemini API) To recognise foods and estimate nutrition
AI food search The text you type Google (Gemini API) To estimate nutrition values
Barcode scan & product search The scanned barcode or search term Open Food Facts To look up product and nutrition data
Crash & error reporting Technical diagnostics (error type, stack trace, app version, device model, OS version) Sentry (EU / Germany region) To detect and fix crashes and bugs

Google (Gemini API)

AI features are powered by Google’s Generative Language (Gemini) API. When you use an AI feature, the relevant data described above is transmitted to Google for processing and returned to your device. Google acts as a processor for this request. Please refer to Google’s Privacy Policy and the Gemini API Terms for details on how Google handles API data.

Open Food Facts

Barcode lookups and product searches query the public Open Food Facts database. Only the barcode or search term is sent; no personal entries are included.

Sentry (crash reporting)

We use Sentry to collect crash and error diagnostics so we can fix problems. Sentry is configured in its EU (Germany) region. We disable the collection of personal identifiers, and we drop console log breadcrumbs so that the content of your entries (such as diary or meal text) is not included in error reports. Reports contain technical information such as the error, a stack trace, the app version and the device/OS. See Sentry’s Privacy Policy.

4. Device permissions

The App may ask for the following permissions. You can grant or deny them, and change them later in your device settings. Data from these sensors is used on your device; it is only transmitted if it is part of an AI feature you trigger (e.g. a food photo).

  • Camera — to scan barcodes and take photos of meals.
  • Photo library — to select a meal photo.
  • Microphone — to record voice notes in the diary.
  • Motion & Fitness / Health data (steps) — to read your step count for activity and calorie estimates. Read on-device only.
  • Location (GPS) — to track outdoor workouts (route, distance, pace). Stored locally on your device.
  • Notifications — for local reminders (e.g. habits, tasks, diary). These are on-device notifications; no data is sent to us.

5. What we do not do

  • We do not sell your data.
  • We do not use advertising or advertising trackers.
  • We do not create user profiles for marketing.
  • We do not store your diary, health or nutrition content on our servers.

6. Legal bases (GDPR)

  • Performance of the service you request (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): sending data to Google or Open Food Facts when you actively use an AI or lookup feature.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR): crash and error diagnostics to keep the App stable and secure.

7. Data retention

Your entries remain on your device until you delete them or uninstall the App. Data sent to Google or Open Food Facts is processed to fulfil the request and retained according to those providers’ policies. Crash reports at Sentry are retained according to Sentry’s retention settings and then deleted.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict and port your personal data, and to object to processing. Because your content lives on your device, you can exercise most of these directly:

  • Access & portability: use the in-app export function.
  • Erasure: delete entries in the App, or uninstall the App to remove all local data.

For any request, or to contact the supervisory authority, you can reach us at nodal.dev.official@gmail.com. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority.

9. Children

The App is not directed at children under 16. If you believe a child has provided data through the App, please contact us.

10. International transfers

Some processors (e.g. Google) may process data outside the EU/EEA. Where this occurs, appropriate safeguards such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses apply, as described in the respective provider’s policy. Sentry is used in its EU (Germany) region.
